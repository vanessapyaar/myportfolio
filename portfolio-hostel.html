Eco Hostel Project

Check the live site on GitHub

The hostel website was first built in 2011. With all the web changes and rapid innovations throughout the years, they decided to change the look and feel of the site, back in 2015. The main goal was to improve the user experience to generate more leads and direct bookings. Also, they wanted to adjust the branding to better match their client's profile.


The Problem

The main problem of this website was the low conversion rate and lack of responsiveness resulting in a poor user experience and high bounce rate.

The hospitality field faces a big challenge when it comes to revenue generation. The big hotel chains retain most of the market share, hence small business needs to rely on third-party vendors to generate bookings, which costs about 18% of each reservation.

Before



Process and Approaches

During the briefing, I gathered information with the business owners and clients, to empathize and define the pain points. I also analyzed the data on google analytics to evaluate metrics such as bounce rate, returning visitors, and the general engagement.

The first thing that caught my attention while navigating the site was the lack of responsiveness for mobile devices. A relevant fraction of users were coming from this source.

With heatmaps, we were able to understand that users were clicking in low conversion elements and leaving the site without relevant actions.

Lastly, I conducted a research with clients to verify the cohesiveness of the branding with their profile. With the results in hand, I suggested a logo redesign since most of the guests did not understand the meaning of the existing one.

I wanted to see all the marketing and printed material in harmony, delivering the same message and speaking the same language.

Design Solution

With the concept of the hostel in mind (built from data analysis and clients feedback) combined with business goals, I presented a design solution that was aligned with both - business and clients.

A solution that would impact not only the user experience but also the conversion rate and lead generation.



Results

After the final designs being implemented, I suggested to run an A/B test with the purpose of fine tuning buttons placements for the booking page. We found a variation that was 12% more engaging.

With the new design, we improved direct bookings and conversion rates by 20% and as a bonus the leads from contact form increased in 8%.
