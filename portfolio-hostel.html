Eco Hostel Project

Check the live site on GitHub

The Eco Hostel website was created for a client based out of Porto Alegre, Brazil. They decided to change the appearance of the site in order to improve the user experience and generate more direct bookings. In addition, they wanted to adjust the brand to better match their customer's profile. For a first time developer, I found it challenging but rewarding to create a website that pleased the client. From developing the content to coordinating the user research, I believe this site displays my knowledge of front-end development.


Project Background

The main problem of this website was the low conversion rate and lack of responsiveness resulting in a poor user experience and high bounce rate.

The hospitality field faces a big challenge when it comes to revenue generation. The big hotel chains retain most of the market share, hence small business needs to rely on third-party vendors to generate bookings, which costs about 18% of each reservation.

Process & Approaches

During the briefing, I collected information with owners and clients, to empathize and define the pain points. I also analyzed the data on Google Analytics to evaluate metrics like bounce rate, returning visitors, and the overall engagement.

The first thing that caught my attention while browsing the site was the lack of responsiveness for mobile devices. A relevant fraction of users come from this source.

With heatmaps, we were able to understand that users were clicking in low conversion elements and leaving the site without relevant actions.

Finally, I conducted surveys with customers to verify the cohesion of the brand with the customer's profile. With the results in hand, I suggested a redesign of the logo, as most guests did not understand the meaning of the existing one.

My goal was to have all the marketing and printed material in harmony, conveying the same message and speaking the same language.

Solution

With hostel concept in mind (built from data analysis and customer feedback) combined with business objectives, I presented a design solution aligned with business and customers.

A solution that would impact not only the user experience, but also the conversion rate and lead generation.



Final UI Design

After implementing the final designs, I suggested running an A/B test (Optimizely) in order to adjust button placements for the booking page. We found 12% more engaging variation.

With the new design, we improved direct bookings and conversion rates by 17% and, as a bonus the leads from contact form increased by 8%.
