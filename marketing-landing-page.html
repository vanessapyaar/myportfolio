Automobile Pricing Page Redesign

This is a redesign of a automotive classified website, where I redesigned the pricing page to a modern version, focusing on engagement and conversion rate.


Project Background


The main source of revenue of this classified website comes from clients placing their advertisements (cars for sale). These advertisements, created by paying customers, will turn into listings to populate the portal's search results page. The pricing page is where it all starts: the customers need to choose a plan from three options, and from there they will enter a conversion funnel process.

The Problem

This landing page needed a redesign because the conversion rate was below average for both desktop and mobile devices. The customers were complaining about the confusion between the packages and the lack of responsiveness for handheld devices.

Pricing Page - Before

Goals & Objectives

The goal of this project was to redesign the pricing page to increase conversion. The main source of income of this automobile portal comes from the listings (advertisements) bought by users trying to sell their car.

My Approach

My first step while working on this project was to gather data, analyze heatmaps and understand the users' behavior while on the pricing page. I wanted to understand main areas of clicks, and the scrolling depth of that page. That would clarify the direction of my new design. I also studied the data on Google Analytics to understand demographics, preferences, bounce rate, conversion, to have a holistic view. It's a good practice to combine quantitative and qualitative data. To verify the success of the redesign we would run a split A/B testing, redirecting 50% of the users to the new pricing page and compare the metris of baseline (old design) versus the redesign. In the wireframig stage we wanted to verify the amount of real estate each step of the process would take on both desktop and mobile. We also created prototypes to run usability testing and verify our clients' response to a multi step process versus a single page. We recorded the sessions and only 10% of the users showed a preference towards the single page.
