Personal Finance App

Financial education is the type of knowledge that allows an individual to make informed and effective decisions with his financial resources.


The Problem


Research by the Financial Industry Regulatory Authority states that "63% of Americans are financially illiterate." More than half of all Americans would experience financial hardship if they had to cover an emergency expense of $1000 or less in the next 30 days.

Goals & Objectives

The goal was to build a money management application in which the user could insert their daily expenses and see the monthly budget, balancing income and expenses. The app needed to provide reminders for the user to enter the daily expenses. Visual cues represented in graphical reports to guide the user were also expected in this design challenge.

My Process

I decided to structure my process following the design thinking approach. To understand users, challenge my assumptions, redefine problems and create an intuitive financial app to prototype and test.



User Reseach

While researching, I discovered that 80% of Americans are caught up in the chains of debt. To get a clear vision of how significant is this number: if you go for a walk now and count the first 10 people you see, 8 of those 10 people are in debt.
I interviewed a diversity of users, from distinct ethnic groups and identified two major pain points: lack of discipline and poor financial education.





High Fidelity Designs

I wanted to focus on the major pain points, so I emphasized the notifications to remind the user to entry data while providing visual education to the current expenses. The rewards were also a focal point, to make good use of positive reinforcement.
